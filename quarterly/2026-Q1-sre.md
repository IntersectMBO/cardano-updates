## 2026-01 - 2026-03

### Main achievements

In addition to ongoing general maintenance and support of cardano environments,
some highlights of SRE achievements for this quarter include:

* All IOE cardano-parts supported node environments, including preview, preprod,
  and mainnet, were upgraded through cardano-node releases `10.5.4` and `10.6.2`,
  with iterative pre-release candidate deployments of `10.7.0` tested throughout
  the quarter. New `kes-agent` and `dmq-node` binaries were integrated for node
  `10.7.0`, and CI tests for consensus db-tooling were added to ensure
  bundled version compatibility.

* An internal Dijkstra network was established for intra-era hard fork testing,
  respun with genesis parameter optimizations, and the van Rossem PV11 cost model
  was submitted, ratified, and enacted in preparation for Protocol Version 11.
  Demo and environment scripts were substantially improved with PV11 intra-era
  hard fork support, Constitutional Committee key voting, DRep registration, and
  constitution adoption.

* The trace dispatcher was migrated out of cardano-node into its own repository,
  [hermod-tracing](https://github.com/IntersectMBO/hermod-tracing). CI monitoring
  and alerting infrastructure was significantly expanded with Mimir Alertmanager,
  Loki ruler-based alerts, and Loki log shipping deployed to playground and
  mainnet environments.

* The SRE team provided significant operational support for the launch of the
  [USDCx service on Cardano](https://www.iog.io/news/usdcx-on-cardano-is-live-a-new-chapter-for-stablecoin-utility),
  including infrastructure and monitoring contributions.

* Darwin CI infrastructure received substantial upgrades including version bumps,
  guest VM lifecycle management, and improved alerting. Additional
  `x86_64-linux` build farm machines were added to the Hydra cluster to increase
  capacity.

* Some cloud resources were relocated to more stable regions after disruption
  due to conflict in the Middle East.

Details for this and many other SRE work items can be found in the biweekly SRE
reports.
